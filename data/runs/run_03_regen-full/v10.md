# Corporate Cybersecurity Policy

## 1. Authentication Policy

### Purpose
Establish secure authentication methods to protect our assets and enhance productivity. Strong authentication reduces risks and improves overall security posture.

### Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and systems requiring authentication. Covers all corporate applications and external partner integrations.

### Directives
Use MFA for all critical systems. Minimum password length: 12 characters. Passwords must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Rotate passwords quarterly. Track password history for the last five passwords. Store credentials using Cryptographic Salt. Implement OIDC for modern applications and SAML for legacy systems. Require Biometric verification for high-security access. Define SLA for identity provider uptime.

### Exceptions
Legacy systems may use single-factor authentication temporarily. Document exceptions and review monthly. Isolated systems may use local authentication; document and review annually.

### Enforcement
Violations reported to the security team. Penalties may include access revocation after three violations within six months. Escalation to management occurs within 48 hours.

## 2. Network Access Control

### Purpose
Restrict network access to authorized personnel, enhancing security and operational efficiency. Implementing Zero Trust architecture minimizes risks and protects sensitive data.

### Scope
Covers all network infrastructure and connected devices. Applies to all personnel accessing the network.

### Directives
Implement RBAC for all access decisions. Use Bastion Host for administrative access. Segment networks by sensitivity. Monitor with Endpoint Telemetry to detect anomalies.

### Exceptions
Emergency access allowed with post-incident review. See Section 1 for details.

### Enforcement
Access logs reviewed weekly. Unauthorized access triggers investigation. Penalties may include access restrictions for up to 30 days.

---

## 3. Data Protection

### Purpose
Safeguard sensitive data throughout its lifecycle, ensuring compliance and protecting our reputation. Effective data protection enhances customer trust and business value.

### Scope
Applies to all data classified as sensitive or higher. Covers all systems storing or processing sensitive data.

### Directives
Encrypt Data At Rest using AES-256. Encrypt Data In Transit using TLS 1.3. Implement PKI for certificate management. Follow GDPR data handling requirements.

### Exceptions
Test environments may use relaxed encryption. Production data must never be in test environments.

### Enforcement
Quarterly audits ensure compliance. Non-compliance escalated to the CISO within 72 hours. Penalties may include fines up to $5,000.

---

## 4. Incident Response

### Purpose
Establish a rapid response to security incidents, minimizing damage and ensuring business continuity. Effective incident response protects our assets and reputation.

### Scope
Covers all security events and all personnel involved in the response process. Applies to all departments.

### Directives
Deploy SIEM for centralized logging. Maintain Honeypot systems for threat detection. Follow Least Privilege during investigations. Use Air Gap for forensic analysis.

### Exceptions
None. All incidents require a documented response.

### Enforcement
Post-incident reviews are mandatory. Lessons learned must be documented within 14 days. Non-compliance may result in penalties up to $2,500.

---

## 5. Cloud Storage Security

### Purpose
Ensure secure cloud storage practices to protect sensitive data and maintain compliance. Effective cloud security enhances operational efficiency and reduces risks.

### Scope
Applies to all cloud storage solutions used by the organization. Covers all data stored in the cloud.

### Directives
Encrypt all data stored in the cloud. Implement access controls based on RBAC. Regularly review cloud storage configurations for compliance with SOC2 requirements.

### Exceptions
Temporary exceptions may be granted for specific projects. Document and review exceptions quarterly.

### Enforcement
Compliance audits conducted every six months. Non-compliance may result in penalties up to $3,000. Escalation to management occurs within 30 days.
