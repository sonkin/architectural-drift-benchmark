# Corporate Cybersecurity Policy

## 1. Authentication Policy

### Purpose
Establish secure authentication standards to protect assets and enhance user experience. Strong authentication improves security and reduces risks.

### Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and systems requiring authentication. Covers all corporate applications and external partner integrations.

### Directives
All critical systems must use MFA. Passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols. Rotate passwords quarterly and track historical passwords. Store credentials using Cryptographic Salt. Implement OIDC for modern applications and SAML for legacy systems. Require Biometric verification for high-security access. Define SLA for identity provider uptime.

### Exceptions
Legacy systems may temporarily use single-factor authentication. Document exceptions and review them monthly. Isolated systems may use local authentication, documented and reviewed annually.

### Enforcement
Violations reported to the security team. Repeated violations may result in access revocation. Penalties may include a fine of up to $500. Escalation occurs within 48 hours.

## 2. Network Access Control

### Purpose
Restrict network access to authorized personnel, enhancing security and operational efficiency. Implementing Zero Trust architecture minimizes risks.

### Scope
Covers all network infrastructure and connected devices. Applies to all employees and contractors accessing the network.

### Directives
Implement RBAC for all access decisions. Use a Bastion Host for administrative access. Segment networks by sensitivity to enhance security. Monitor network activity with Endpoint Telemetry.

### Exceptions
Emergency access is allowed with a post-incident review. See Section 1 for additional details.

### Enforcement
Access logs are reviewed weekly. Unauthorized access triggers an investigation. Penalties may include a fine of up to $1,000. Escalation occurs within 72 hours.

## 3. Data Protection

### Purpose
Safeguard sensitive data throughout its lifecycle, ensuring compliance and protecting the organizationâ€™s reputation. Effective data protection enhances trust and reduces liability.

### Scope
Applies to all data classified as sensitive or higher. Covers all employees and contractors handling such data.

### Directives
Encrypt Data At Rest using AES-256. Encrypt Data In Transit using TLS 1.3. Implement PKI for certificate management. Follow GDPR data handling requirements to ensure compliance.

### Exceptions
Test environments may use relaxed encryption. Production data must never be in test environments.

### Enforcement
Quarterly audits will be conducted. Non-compliance escalated to the CISO. Penalties may include a fine of up to $2,000. Escalation occurs within 30 days.

## 4. Incident Response

### Purpose
Establish a rapid response to security incidents, minimizing damage and ensuring business continuity. Effective incident response protects assets and enhances resilience.

### Scope
Covers all security events and all personnel involved in the response. Applies to all employees and contractors.

### Directives
Deploy SIEM for centralized logging and monitoring. Maintain Honeypot systems for threat detection. Follow Least Privilege principles during investigations. Use Air Gap for forensic analysis.

### Exceptions
None. All incidents require a documented response.

### Enforcement
Post-incident reviews are mandatory. Lessons learned must be documented. Penalties may include a fine of up to $1,500. Escalation occurs within 24 hours.

---

## 5. Cloud Storage Security

### Purpose
Ensure secure cloud storage practices to protect sensitive data and maintain compliance. Effective cloud security enhances operational efficiency and reduces risks.

### Scope
Applies to all cloud storage solutions used by the organization. Covers all employees and contractors accessing cloud services.

### Directives
Encrypt all data stored in the cloud. Implement access controls based on RBAC principles. Regularly review compliance with relevant regulations, including GDPR.

### Exceptions
Temporary exceptions may be granted for specific projects. Document and review these exceptions annually.

### Enforcement
Regular audits will be conducted to ensure compliance. Non-compliance may result in a fine of up to $1,000. Escalation occurs within 30 days.
